## Cantina De Luca & Tenuta del Sole

### Overview
**Tenuta del Sole** ("Estate of the Sun") is the De Luca family vineyard, nestled in the rolling hills of Tuscany, not far from the historic town of Montalcino. The estate takes its name from the way the morning sun strikes the terraced slopes, transforming them into fields of gold, making it seem as if the land itself is glowing.

The vineyard spans fifteen hectares, divided into distinct plots, each with its own name, its own character, its own story. The soil is a mix of clay and limestone, perfect for Sangiovese, the grape that has made Tuscany famous. But the De Lucas also grow other varietals â€” Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, small plots of experimental grapes that Bianca tends with particular care.

### The Named Plots

#### Il Giardino del Nonno (Grandfather's Garden)
The oldest plot, planted by Giuseppe De Luca in 1892. The vines here are gnarled and ancient, their roots reaching deep into the earth, their grapes small but intensely flavored. This is where Roberto learned to prune, where Bianca learned to taste, where generations of De Lucas have learned to respect the land.

#### La Collina del Vento (The Windy Hill)
A plot that catches the afternoon breeze, its name coming from the way the wind seems to dance through the rows, keeping the grapes dry and healthy. The wine from here has a particular freshness, a liveliness that Bianca attributes to the constant air movement.

#### Il Campo del Sole (The Field of Sun)
The largest plot, facing south, catching the sun from dawn to dusk. This is where the most consistent grapes grow, where the harvest is always reliable, where the wine is always good. But Roberto says that "good" is not enough â€” he wants "extraordinary," and extraordinary comes from attention, from care, from treating each vine as an individual.

#### La Terrazza degli Ulivi (The Terrace of Olives)
A small plot surrounded by ancient olive trees, their silver leaves providing dappled shade. The grapes here are smaller, more concentrated, protected from the harsh afternoon sun. The wine has a particular elegance, a refinement that Roberto attributes to the careful balance of sun and shadow.

#### Il Sentiero della Memoria (The Path of Memory)
A narrow plot that follows an old footpath, once used by shepherds. There's something special about this place â€” a sense of history, of continuity, of the generations that have walked here before. The wine from this plot always has a note that Roberto cannot name, something between earth and memory, between past and present.

#### Il Vigneto della Nonna (Grandmother's Vineyard)
Named for Maria De Luca, Roberto and Bianca's grandmother, who planted these vines with her own hands in 1955. This was her favorite plot, the one she tended with particular care, the one where she felt most connected to the land. The wine from here carries her memory, her love, her spirit.

But over the years, additions have been made â€” a modern fermentation room, temperature-controlled storage, a laboratory where Roberto can analyze the wine's chemistry. **Summer:** The vines grow, the grapes form, the sun works its magic. The days are long and hot, the work constant. This is when patience is tested, when the balance between nature and nurture becomes clear, when the De Lucas spend most of their time in the vineyard, tending, watching, waiting.

**Autumn:** Harvest â€” the culmination of a year's work. The air fills with the scent of crushed grapes, the sound of laughter and conversation. This is when the family gathers, when friends come to help, when work becomes celebration, when the cantina comes alive with activity.

**Winter:** The vines sleep, the work shifts to the cellar. This is when wine is made, when time and care transform grapes into something more.
